Greg Karpovsky Adverse News Report

January 3, 2025

Key Findings Summary

  • Greg Karpovsky (Russia/UK) — Founded Eurokommerz in 2000, collapsed 2008 with $1B+ debt amid fraud allegations. HBK lawsuit claimed "many fictitious clients" and "significantly inflated" financials
  • Greg Karpovsky (UK) — Founded Stenn Technologies, entered administration Dec 2024 after HSBC discovered fake client relationships and links to Russian money laundering
  • Stenn Assets UK — Received $1.7M in Oct 2020 from Singapore company linked to Feliks Medvedev's $150M+ money laundering operation
  • Greg Karpovsky — Personal email listed in US indictment linked to trading account used in Medvedev's laundering scheme
  • Silverbird Global (UK) — Karpovsky was director/shareholder since July 2021; entered special administration Mar 2024 after Russian sanctions breaches discovered
  • Eurokommerz — Control transferred to structures linked to Mukhtar Ablyazov (fugitive Kazakh oligarch) in 2008; used shell company guarantors
  • Stenn Technologies — Major firms including OMV, Repsol, Zalando, Japanese and Taiwanese corporates denied any relationship with company
  • Stenn Technologies — Received hundreds of millions from Serbian shell companies mimicking names of major corporations
  • Greg Karpovsky — Left UK before Dec 4 administration call; did not appear when 200+ employees made redundant; whereabouts unknown
  • Stenn Creditors — Face $194M deficiency: Stenn Assets UK £65M, Stenn International £91M. HSBC owed £28M
  • EY — Resigned as Stenn auditor in 2018 citing concerns about "certain related party transactions" and inadequate management explanations
  • UK FRC — Investigating Azets (2017-2022) and Deloitte (2023) audits of Stenn following suspicious transaction allegations
  • Stenn Suppliers — Traced to hollowed buildings in Prague; one supplier (Oleg Gerdt) OFAC-sanctioned for aiding Russian naval intelligence
  • Greg Karpovsky — Interfactor — Greg Karpovsky claimed a “successful exit” from the Interfactor digital factoring platform (2010–2015), with no public documentation of exit terms, acquirer, or circumstances.
