January 3, 2025

Key Affected Directors & Related Persons Summary

  • Christopher Rigby — Served as Group CFO and CIO through the entire period when Stenn allegedly relied on non‑genuine receivables, culminating in insolvency, large creditor losses and multiple regulatory and audit investigations.
  • Maxim Faldin — Russian national who was CEO of Silverbird during its Russia‑sanctions‑driven collapse and then joined Stenn as CCO, linking him to two failed, Russia‑linked fintechs in quick succession.
  • Fred Destin — Faced past inappropriate‑behaviour allegations that harmed fundraising and later served as a director of Silverbird when it collapsed into special administration over sanctions issues.
  • Andrey Polevoy — Founder‑era executive and managing director during key Stenn growth years when suspicious transactions occurred, stepping down before the main fraud allegations and insolvency.
  • Hans de Zwart — Managing director and Luxembourg securitisation executive who resigned before collapse, yet was part of governance during the build‑up of the problematic securitisation structures.
  • Andrey Gurdzhibek — COO described as Karpovsky's top lieutenant, present at crisis meetings and publicly denying wrongdoing despite extensive evidence of bogus suppliers and irregular counterparties.
  • Hubert Larenaudie — Appointed President of Stenn in 2023 to drive scale just before the fraud revelations and collapse, raising questions about oversight and commercial governance.
  • Georgy Babilashvili — Co‑founder and director of Silverbird during the period when Russia‑sanctions breaches were identified.
  • Colin Lynch & Joshua Eaton — Former Stenn directors who exited before the collapse but sat on entities later central to the insolvent structure.
